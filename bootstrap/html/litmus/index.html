Whether you have a simple blog or a globally distributed application, Litmus can help you analyze its performance and availability from around the world.

Litmus monitors your internal and external network performance and uptime, so that you can make your users happier.

Building on Grafana, we have created a gorgeously powerful, fully open platform to allow you to track and troubleshoot your application performance and availability

pinpoint the problem quickly

Is the problem a regional network issue? DNS, yet again? A slowdown in the backend?

Litmus allows you to pinpoint the problem through gorgeous visualizations, giving you context across different layers of the stack.

a global footprint you make your own

Monitor performance from all over the planet, across different networks and carriers with dozens of public Litmus collectors.

Install our open-source Collector software to measure the performance of your own datacenters and offices, and customize your footprint.

Get the complete picturte both inside and outside your network.

reduce the noise

Litmus provides a clean backbone signal from around the world, and automatically learns what “normal” is so that you don’t have to.

RAINDEX, our dynamically computed index of performance from every Collector make your alerts even even cleaner and more actionable.

collaborate with anyone

Litmus makes it easy to share your data with your team, partners, or the w ke a snapshot for sharing it with anyone outside your company, giving them access to just the data that you want.

get alerted

Sit back and relax, we’ll let you know when you experience a problem, and won’t annoy you with false positives.

open source without the tradeoffs

Take back control of your data destiny, and avoid lock-in. Litmus is 100% open-source, and also available as a fully supported SaaS and on-premise offering from raintank.

ridiculously easy to get started

There’s no software or agent to install or configure. Be up and running with Litmus and Grafana in less than 60 seconds.

Just give us the domain name of your site or application, and we’ll automatically detect what to monitor.
